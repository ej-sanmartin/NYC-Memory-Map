Great Places with Great Meaning to Me

Welcome to My Neighborhood

New York City has had a long history. First being occupied by various Native American tribes and then being colonized by the Dutch in the 17th century, it has gone through a large transformation to what we come to know, today. New York City started at Manhattan Island's most Southern tip as a harbor and has grown exponentially to the grand city that we know today.

It expanded Northward and the Upper East Side, where I call home, has been growing since the early 19th century.

I have great love for this neighborhood. From the parks that line FDR Drive to the fancy restaurant that serve to empty my stomach as it fulls my belly. The people are friendly and lively and I am so happy to call this place home.

The map displays all the places I frequent in the city and even has a few spots that are not in the Upper East Side. These places mean a lot to me and have made my time in New York City memorable.
